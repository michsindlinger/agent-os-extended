# Content Writing Best Practices

Apply proven copywriting formulas, persuasion techniques, and conversion-focused writing to create compelling landing pages and ad campaigns.

## 1. Copywriting Formulas

### AIDA Formula (Attention, Interest, Desire, Action)

**Structure**:
1. **Attention**: Grab with bold headline
2. **Interest**: Hook with relevant problem
3. **Desire**: Build want with benefits
4. **Action**: Clear CTA

**Example** (Invoice automation):
```
[Attention]
Headline: "Freelancers: Stop Losing Money to Forgotten Invoices"

[Interest]
Subheadline: "You're leaving ‚Ç¨500/month on the table.
Every forgotten invoice is money you'll never see."

[Desire]
Body: "InvoiceSnap automatically reminds you to invoice.
1-click generation from your time tracking.
Get paid faster, work less."

[Action]
CTA: "Start Recovering Lost Income ‚Üí"
```

### PAS Formula (Problem, Agitate, Solution)

**Structure**:
1. **Problem**: Identify the pain
2. **Agitate**: Make it worse (amplify emotion)
3. **Solution**: Present your product as relief

**Example** (Invoice automation):
```
[Problem]
"Creating invoices manually takes 2 hours every week."

[Agitate]
"That's 8 hours per month. 96 hours per year.
At ‚Ç¨50/hour, you're wasting ‚Ç¨4,800 in billable time.
Plus the stress. Plus the mistakes. Plus late payments."

[Solution]
"InvoiceSnap generates perfect invoices in 60 seconds.
Reclaim those 96 hours. Never forget an invoice again."
```

### Before-After-Bridge (BAB)

**Structure**:
1. **Before**: Current painful state
2. **After**: Desired future state
3. **Bridge**: How product gets them there

**Example**:
```
[Before]
"You spend Sunday evenings wrestling with Excel,
creating invoices by hand, double-checking calculations."

[After]
"Imagine clicking one button on Friday afternoon,
and all your invoices are sent. Weekend starts early."

[Bridge]
"InvoiceSnap connects to your time tracking.
One click ‚Üí Invoice generated ‚Üí Sent to client.
It's that simple."
```

### FAB Formula (Features, Advantages, Benefits)

**Framework**:
- **Feature**: What it is (technical)
- **Advantage**: What it does (functional)
- **Benefit**: What it means for user (emotional)

**Example**:
```
Feature: "AI-powered invoice generation"
Advantage: "Creates invoices in 60 seconds instead of 30 minutes"
Benefit: "Spend your evenings with family, not spreadsheets"

Feature: "Automatic payment reminders"
Advantage: "Sends reminders 7 days before due date"
Benefit: "Get paid on time, stop chasing clients"
```

**Always lead with benefit**:
‚ùå "Our tool has AI-powered generation" (feature-first)
‚úÖ "Get your evenings back with 60-second invoicing" (benefit-first)

## 2. Value Proposition Design

### Value Proposition Formula

**Template**:
```
[Product] helps [target audience] [achieve outcome] by [unique approach].
```

**Examples**:
```
‚úÖ "InvoiceSnap helps freelance creatives get paid faster by automating invoices from time tracking."

‚úÖ "Slack helps teams communicate better by replacing email with organized channels."

‚ùå "InvoiceSnap is an invoicing tool with many features."
   (No target audience, no outcome, no differentiator)
```

### Unique Value Proposition (UVP)

**Components**:
1. **Headline**: Main benefit (5-10 words)
2. **Subheadline**: Who it's for, what it does (10-20 words)
3. **Differentiation**: How it's different (5-10 words)

**Example** (Invoice automation):
```
Headline: "From Timesheet to Invoice in 60 Seconds"
Subheadline: "The simplest invoicing tool for freelancers who hate accounting"
Differentiation: "No setup, no complexity, ‚Ç¨5/month"
```

**UVP Testing**:
- Show to 10 target users
- Ask: "What does this product do?" (test clarity)
- Ask: "Would this be useful?" (test relevance)
- Refine based on misunderstandings

### Differentiation Articulation

**Formula**:
```
Unlike [competitor], [product] [unique advantage].
```

**Examples**:
```
‚úÖ "Unlike QuickBooks, InvoiceSnap requires zero accounting knowledge."
‚úÖ "Unlike FreshBooks, InvoiceSnap costs ‚Ç¨5/month, not ‚Ç¨25."
```

**Common Differentiators**:
- **Speed**: "10x faster than [competitor]"
- **Simplicity**: "No training required, unlike [competitor]"
- **Price**: "5x cheaper than [competitor]"
- **Niche**: "Built for [specific audience], not [broad audience]"
- **Quality**: "99.99% uptime vs. [competitor's] 95%"

## 3. Call-to-Action Optimization

### Action Verb Usage

**Strong Action Verbs**:
- **Get**: "Get Started", "Get Your Free Trial"
- **Start**: "Start Saving Time", "Start Free Trial"
- **Join**: "Join 10,000 Users", "Join the Waitlist"
- **Discover**: "Discover How", "See How It Works"
- **Claim**: "Claim Your Discount", "Claim Free Access"
- **Try**: "Try It Free", "Try Risk-Free"

**Weak Verbs to Avoid**:
‚ùå "Submit", "Enter", "Click Here", "Learn More"

**Before/After Examples**:
```
‚ùå "Submit" ‚Üí ‚úÖ "Get Early Access"
‚ùå "Learn More" ‚Üí ‚úÖ "See How It Works"
‚ùå "Click Here" ‚Üí ‚úÖ "Start Free Trial"
```

### Urgency Creation

**Scarcity Tactics**:
- **Limited Quantity**: "Only 50 spots left"
- **Limited Time**: "Offer ends Friday"
- **Exclusive Access**: "Early access for first 100 users"

**Examples**:
```
‚úÖ "Join the waitlist (200 spots remaining)"
‚úÖ "50% off for first 100 customers"
‚úÖ "Beta access closes in 3 days"

‚ùå "Limited time offer!" (vague)
‚ùå "Act now!" (fake urgency)
```

**Urgency Without Being Pushy**:
```
Natural: "We're launching with 100 beta users to ensure quality onboarding"
Pushy: "BUY NOW OR MISS OUT FOREVER!!!"
```

### Specificity in CTAs

**Vague vs. Specific**:
```
‚ùå Vague: "Sign Up"
‚úÖ Specific: "Start Free 14-Day Trial"

‚ùå Vague: "Get Started"
‚úÖ Specific: "Get Your First Invoice in 60 Seconds"

‚ùå Vague: "Join Us"
‚úÖ Specific: "Join 5,000+ Freelancers"
```

**Specificity Increases Trust**:
```
Generic: "Save time and money"
Specific: "Save 5 hours per week, ‚Ç¨50 per month"
‚Üí 2x higher conversion (specific wins)
```

### Button Text Best Practices

**Length**: 2-5 words ideal
```
‚úÖ "Start Free Trial" (3 words)
‚úÖ "Get Instant Access" (3 words)
‚ùå "Click here to sign up for our amazing product trial" (11 words, too long)
```

**First-Person vs. Second-Person**:
```
First-Person (higher conversion): "Get My Free Trial"
Second-Person (lower conversion): "Get Your Free Trial"

‚Üí First-person feels more personal
```

**Value Reinforcement**:
```
Standard: "Sign Up"

Standard: "Start Trial"
Value-Added: "Start Free Trial (No Credit Card)"
```

**Pattern**: Ask the pain point question

**Examples**:

**Answer Immediately**:

### Headline Testing Checklist

- [ ] Benefit clear in 3 seconds? (pass the blink test)

## 5. Ad Copy Structure

### Google Ads Character Limits

**Search Ads**:
- **Headlines**: 30 characters max (up to 15 headlines)
- **Descriptions**: 90 characters max (up to 4 descriptions)
- Google shows: 3 headlines + 2 descriptions (rotated)

**Display Ads**:
- **Short headline**: 30 characters
- **Long headline**: 90 characters
- **Description**: 90 characters

### Meta Ads (Facebook/Instagram) Character Limits

**Feed Ads**:
- **Primary Text**: 125 characters (desktop), more on mobile
- **Headline**: 27 characters (40 on Facebook)
- **Description**: 27 characters

**Story Ads**:
- Text overlays only (use minimal text, let visual speak)

### Ad Copy Formula: Hook, Body, CTA

**Structure**:
```
[Hook] - Grab attention in first 3 words
[Body] - Expand benefit, address objection
[CTA] - Clear next step
```

**Google Search Ad Example**:
```
Headline 1: "Invoice in 60 Seconds" (22 chars) [HOOK]
Headline 2: "For Freelance Creatives" (23 chars) [AUDIENCE]
Headline 3: "‚Ç¨5/Month, No Setup" (18 chars) [OBJECTION]

Description 1: "Stop wasting hours on manual invoicing. Auto-generate from time tracking." (77) [BODY]
Description 2: "Join 5,000+ freelancers. Start free trial, no credit card required." (68) [CTA + TRUST]
```

**Facebook Feed Ad Example**:
```
Primary Text: "Freelance creatives: recover ‚Ç¨500/month from forgotten invoices. InvoiceSnap reminds you + generates invoices in 1 click." (125 chars)

Headline: "Invoice in 60 Seconds" (24 chars)

CTA Button: "Sign Up" (preset button)
```

### Ad Copy Variants (Testing)

**What to Test** (7-10 variants):
1. **Pain-Focused vs. Gain-Focused**
   - A: "Stop losing ‚Ç¨500/month" (pain)
   - B: "Earn ‚Ç¨500 more per month" (gain)

2. **Feature vs. Benefit**
   - A: "AI-powered invoice generation"
   - B: "Create invoices in 60 seconds"

3. **Question vs. Statement**
   - A: "Tired of manual invoicing?"
   - B: "Automate your invoicing"

4. **Specific vs. General**
   - A: "Save 2 hours every week"
   - B: "Save time on admin work"

5. **Social Proof vs. No Social Proof**
   - A: "Join 5,000+ freelancers"
   - B: "Start invoicing smarter"

6. **Price-Focused vs. Value-Focused**
   - A: "Only ‚Ç¨5 per month"
   - B: "Get paid faster, work less"

7. **Urgency vs. No Urgency**
   - A: "Limited: 50 beta spots left"
   - B: "Start your free trial"

**Test Process**:
```
Week 1: Run all 7 variants
Week 2: Pause bottom 4, keep top 3
Week 3: Create 3 new variants based on top performer
‚Üí Continuous improvement
```

### Ad Copy Checklist

- [ ] Hook in first 3 words ("Freelancers:", "Stop losing", "Save 5 hours")
- [ ] Benefit-driven (not feature-driven)
- [ ] Specific numbers ("60 seconds", "‚Ç¨5/month", "5,000 users")

## 3. Headline Writing

### Headline Formulas

**Formula 1: Number + Adjective + Keyword + Promise**
```
"5 Minute Invoice Creation" (number + time + keyword + promise)
"10 Simple Steps to Faster Payment" (number + adjective + keyword + promise)
```

**Formula 2: How to + Benefit**
```
"How to Get Paid 30% Faster"
"How to Stop Losing Money on Forgotten Invoices"
```

**Formula 3: [Verb] + [Benefit] + Without [Pain]**
```
"Create Invoices Without Accounting Knowledge"
"Get Paid Faster Without Chasing Clients"
```

**Formula 4: The [Adjective] Way to [Benefit]**
```
"The Fastest Way to Get Paid"
"The Simplest Way to Invoice Clients"
```

### Headline Length Guidelines

**Landing Page Headlines**:
- Ideal: 6-12 words
- Max: 20 words
- Must fit on one line (mobile)

**Ad Headlines**:
- Google Search: 30 characters max (4-6 words)
- Facebook: 40 characters recommended (5-8 words)

### Power Words for Headlines

**Urgency**: Now, Today, Instant, Fast, Quick
**Value**: Free, Save, Discount, Bonus
**Exclusivity**: Secret, Exclusive, Members-Only, Limited
**Ease**: Simple, Easy, Effortless, Automatic
**Results**: Proven, Guaranteed, Effective, Reliable

**Example Combinations**:
```
"Get Instant Access" (Get + Instant)
"Start Saving Today" (Start + Save + Today)
"The Simple Way to Get Paid Faster" (Simple + Fast)
```

### Headline A/B Testing

**Test These Variables**:
1. **Length**: Short (5 words) vs. Long (12 words)
2. **Format**: Question vs. Statement
3. **Focus**: Problem vs. Solution
4. **Emotion**: Fear vs. Aspiration
5. **Specificity**: Generic vs. Specific numbers

**Example Test**:
```

```
